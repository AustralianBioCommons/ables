ABLeS is available to research groups and bioinformatics communities across Australia. Read below to determine if your project aligns with our core principles and resource criteria.

To qualify for access, your project must address the following three pillars:


## 1. Domain and impact

> Your work must have a <span class = "emphasis">molecular life sciences focus</span>.

> You are willing to <span class = "emphasis">openly share your results and outputs (exceptions apply)</span>, including data, software, and methods. [**Read more about this here**](ables/sharing.md).


## 2. Technical readiness & responsible resource use

> Your team must have the <span class = "emphasis">"hands-on" bioinformatics skills and expertise</span> required to execute the work, including being prepared to work within either the NCI or Pawsey supercomputing environments.

> You acknowledge that <span class = "emphasis">HPC compute and storage resources are finite</span>, and you have a plan to use them efficiently and within the scope of the project.


## 3. Governance and leadership

> You, or someone in your group, is able and willing to <span class = "emphasis">act as the project lead</span> and be responsible for all resource usage and adherence to facility policies.

> You will <span class = "emphasis">engage with BioCommons and the ABLeS community</span> in an open and collaborative manner.

> You agree to <span class = "emphasis"> a regular reporting and progress review</span> to ensure resources are being utilized effectively.

> All users must comply with the [**NCI Terms and Conditions**](https://nci.org.au/users/nci-terms-and-conditions-access) and [**Pawsey Conditions of Access**](https://pawsey.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/US/overview).
